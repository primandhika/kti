# Minor-to-Moderate Revision Notes

## Explain-to-Learn Microlearning for Metacognitive Regulation in Academic Speaking

### Tujuan Revisi

Bukan mengubah hasil, metode, atau kesimpulan utama, melainkan:

1. Memperkuat kontribusi teoritis penelitian.
2. Mengurangi kesan bahwa peningkatan hanya disebabkan oleh microlearning.
3. Menjelaskan mekanisme pedagogis yang sebenarnya bekerja selama intervensi.
4. Memperkuat positioning artikel untuk reviewer Scopus Q1-Q3.

---

# 1. INTRODUCTION

## Temuan Masalah

Saat ini artikel terlalu menonjolkan microlearning sebagai pusat inovasi.

Padahal berdasarkan desain intervensi, komponen yang lebih berpotensi menjelaskan peningkatan adalah:

* explain-to-learn
* retrieval practice
* repeated oral production
* metacognitive reflection
* absence of slide dependency

Akibatnya reviewer dapat bertanya:

> "Apakah peningkatan terjadi karena microlearning atau karena mahasiswa dipaksa menjelaskan ulang secara intensif?"

---

## BEFORE (verbatim)

> The instructional implication is that microlearning might become more powerful when it is not the end of a learning cycle, but the beginning of one: short input followed by learner explanation followed by explicit reflection on how well that explanation worked.

---

## AFTER (suggested)

> The instructional implication is that microlearning alone may not be sufficient to improve academic speaking. Rather, short instructional input appears most valuable when it initiates a broader generative learning cycle involving learner explanation, retrieval-based oral production, and explicit metacognitive reflection. In this view, microlearning functions as a trigger for active knowledge reconstruction rather than merely a content-delivery format.

---

## Dampak

Kalimat ini memindahkan fokus artikel dari:

"microlearning works"

menjadi:

"microlearning works because it activates generative speaking processes."

---

# 2. INTERVENTION DESIGN

## Temuan Masalah

Bagian ini belum cukup menonjolkan bahwa mahasiswa:

* berbicara tanpa slide
* menjelaskan dengan kata sendiri
* melakukan retrieval
* berlatih secara berulang

Padahal kemungkinan besar di sinilah sumber effect size yang besar.

---

## BEFORE (verbatim)

> students re-explained the material in their own words

---

## AFTER (suggested)

> students re-explained the material entirely in their own words without relying on presentation slides or visual supports. This requirement was intentionally designed to encourage retrieval, spontaneous idea organization, and independent formulation of explanations rather than reading or reproducing prepared content.

---

## Dampak

Reviewer langsung memahami bahwa speaking yang diukur bukan sekadar membaca presentasi.

---

# 3. TAMBAHKAN PENJELASAN RETRIEVAL PRACTICE

## Lokasi

Akhir Section 2.1 Intervention Design

---

## Tambahan Baru

> The oral explanation stage also functioned as a retrieval practice activity. Rather than revisiting information passively, students were required to reconstruct key concepts from memory and communicate them coherently to an imagined audience. Prior research suggests that retrieval-based learning activities strengthen knowledge organization and transfer more effectively than repeated exposure alone. Accordingly, the intervention was designed not only to provide content but also to require active reconstruction of that content through speech.

---

## Dampak

Membuat reviewer melihat desain ini lebih sophisticated daripada sekadar LMS microlearning biasa.

---

# 4. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION

## Temuan Masalah

Hasil sudah bagus tetapi belum cukup mengaitkan effect size dengan mekanisme intervensi.

---

## BEFORE (verbatim)

> When learners repeatedly re-express disciplinary content in their own words, they are compelled to decide what to foreground, what to omit, and whether their framing is coherent for a listener.

---

## AFTER (suggested)

> When learners repeatedly re-express disciplinary content in their own words without the support of presentation slides, they must retrieve, reorganize, and articulate knowledge under conditions that more closely resemble authentic academic communication. This repeated cycle likely strengthened internal representations of content structure, which may explain why gains were particularly pronounced in idea organization and explanatory clarity.

---

## Dampak

Menghubungkan hasil dengan desain secara lebih eksplisit.

---

# 5. STUDENT PERCEPTION SECTION

## Tambahkan Interpretasi

Setelah tema "Explanation as a Metacognitive Trigger"

---

## Tambahan Baru

> Interestingly, many students described moments of difficulty during explanation as productive rather than discouraging. Several participants reported discovering gaps in understanding only when they attempted to explain concepts aloud. This pattern is consistent with generative learning perspectives suggesting that explanation activities reveal incomplete understanding and stimulate deeper processing.

---

## Dampak

Menghubungkan data kualitatif dengan teori generative learning.

---

# 6. LIMITATION SECTION

## Temuan Masalah

Saat ini keterbatasan hanya menekankan desain one-group.

Padahal ada keterbatasan lain yang justru dapat menjadi agenda penelitian berikutnya.

---

## AFTER (additional paragraph)

> Because the intervention combined several instructional components, including microlearning, explanation, retrieval-based speaking practice, and metacognitive reflection, the present design cannot isolate the relative contribution of each element. Future studies employing factorial or comparison-group designs may help determine which components are most influential in producing speaking gains.

---

## Dampak

Reviewer biasanya menyukai penulis yang mengakui kompleksitas intervensinya.

---

# 7. CONCLUSION

## BEFORE (verbatim)

> What appears to matter is what learners are required to do immediately after receiving input.

---

## AFTER (suggested)

> What appears to matter is not merely the brevity of instructional input but the cognitive work that follows it. Learners were required to retrieve, reorganize, explain, and evaluate knowledge repeatedly across eight weeks. Under these conditions, microlearning functioned less as a delivery mechanism and more as the entry point to a structured cycle of generative speaking practice and metacognitive regulation.

---

# Kesimpulan Strategis

Revisi yang paling menguntungkan bukan menambah statistik baru.

Revisi yang paling menguntungkan adalah menggeser positioning artikel dari:

"Microlearning improves speaking"

menjadi:

"Microlearning-initiated explain-to-learn cycles improve speaking through retrieval, generative explanation, and metacognitive regulation."

Posisi kedua lebih kuat secara teoritis, lebih konsisten dengan data yang dilaporkan, dan lebih mudah dipertahankan ketika reviewer mempertanyakan sumber sebenarnya dari peningkatan performa mahasiswa.
