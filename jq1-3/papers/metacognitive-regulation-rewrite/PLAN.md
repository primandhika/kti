## Re-read diagnosis of current article

### Strong elements to retain
- The central problem is clear: academic speaking instruction often emphasizes performance more than the metacognitive processes behind performance.
- The instructional combination is coherent: microlearning provides segmented delivery, and the Feynman technique activates explain-to-learn processing.
- The theoretical anchor is already usable: CTML + SRL.
- The statistical story is clear and strong enough for a concise results section:
  - multivariate gain in metacognitive regulation
  - strongest gains in planning and evaluation
  - large gain in academic speaking performance
  - positive learner perceptions
- The Indonesian higher education relevance is present and should be retained, but more selectively.

### Parts that should be compressed
- The Introduction is currently too expansive and overlaps with the Literature Review.
- The Literature Review repeats points already made in the Introduction, especially about academic speaking, metacognition, microlearning, and the Feynman technique.
- The Discussion repeats numerical findings that are already fully reported in the Results.
- Theoretical tables may be reduced or kept only if they directly improve readability.

### Parts that should be rewritten
- The title may need tightening after the new draft is completed.
- Research questions should be restated more cleanly in the Introduction.
- The Methodology should be rewritten as one compact section without subheadings.
- Results should prioritize the main pattern of findings first, then selective table support.
- Discussion should move from result repetition toward interpretation, contribution, and boundaries of the design.

### Parts to treat cautiously
- Claims that metacognitive regulation is a definitive mediating mechanism are stronger than the reported design clearly supports. The rewrite should use more careful wording unless mediation was directly tested.
- Some references appear recent or convenient; they should be preserved provisionally in the rewrite, but not overstated as verification targets unless checked separately.
- The design is described as design-based research, but the write-up also reads like a pre-post intervention study. The rewrite should acknowledge both the iterative intervention logic and the limits of causal inference.

### Rewrite direction
- Make the article more linear: problem -> gap -> intervention logic -> method -> findings -> interpretation -> implications.
- Treat the current manuscript as source material rather than structure to preserve.
- Build a cleaner draft that can later be adjusted to a target journal.
- Keep the Methodology compact, flowing in paragraph form.
- Reduce table load where possible.
- Preserve the numerical core of the findings where still relevant.
- End with a tighter conclusion that emphasizes contribution without overstating proof.
