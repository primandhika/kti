# Research Design: Assessing Metacognitive Speaking Skills in Indonesian Higher Education

**Based on the methodological framework adapted from Mach (2025)**

## 1. Background and Rationale
Recent evaluations of high-stakes speaking exams (Mach, 2025) demonstrate a systemic bias toward assessing static, declarative knowledge rather than dynamic communicative skills. In the context of Indonesian higher education, particularly in the Bandung and Cimahi regions, a similar risk exists in assessing L1 (Bahasa Indonesia) speaking skills. University courses on speaking—whether general mandatory courses (*MKWK Bahasa Indonesia*) or specialized major courses (*Keterampilan Berbicara* in PBSI/Communication programs)—may inadvertently assess students' **declarative knowledge** of speaking theories (metacognitive knowledge) rather than their active ability to plan, monitor, and regulate their speech in real-time (**metacognitive regulation**).

This study adapts Mach's (2025) document-analysis methodology to investigate the extent to which local higher education assessment practices prioritize declarative knowledge over active metacognitive regulation during practical presentations.

---

## 2. Research Questions (RQs)
Adapted from the original study's focus on cultural content, the RQs for this localized context are:
*   **RQ 1:** To what extent are declarative, knowledge-oriented speaking criteria represented in the practical exam rubrics (UTS/UAS) of higher education institutions in Bandung and Cimahi?
*   **RQ 2:** How effectively do these rubrics integrate criteria for metacognitive regulation (e.g., audience monitoring, breakdown repair, real-time discourse planning) during live presentations?

---

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1. Context and Sampling
*   **Target Sample (Universities):** A focused regional sample of 8 universities, specifically comprising 5 campuses in Cimahi and 3 campuses in Bandung.
*   **Target Population (Students):** First-year students (Semester 1 and 2). The focus includes both general students taking the mandatory curriculum (*MKWK Bahasa Indonesia*) and students in specialized programs such as Indonesian Language and Literature Education (*PBSI*) or Communication sciences taking *Keterampilan Berbicara* (Speaking Skills).
*   **Data Sources:** The primary data source will be the **Practical Exam Grading Rubrics** used during Midterm (*UTS*) and Final (*UAS*) examinations.
*   **Timeline Constraint:** The data collection, analysis, and synthesis are designed to be completed within a strict **4-month timeframe**.

### 3.2. Analytical Framework
The study replaces Larzén’s (2005) cultural typology with a metacognitive framework integrating **Flavell (1979)** (Knowledge & Regulation) and **Goh & Liu** (Metacognitive awareness in speaking). Assessment descriptors in the UTS/UAS rubrics will be coded with a pure focus on cognitive regulation during presentations:

1.  **Metacognitive Knowledge (Declarative / Static):**
    *   *Indicators:* Assessment criteria that reward students for knowing speech structures, stating theories of rhetoric, or identifying communication concepts.
    *   *Keywords in Rubrics:* *Menjelaskan* (explain), *menyebutkan* (state), *mendefinisikan* (define), *memaparkan teori* (present theory).
2.  **Metacognitive Regulation (Dynamic / Skill-Based) [PRIMARY FOCUS]:**
    *   *Planning:* Criteria evaluating how well the student dynamically organizes thoughts and manages time during the presentation.
    *   *Monitoring/Directing:* Criteria evaluating audience engagement, adjusting tone/pace, and maintaining flow based on situational feedback (Goh & Liu).
    *   *Evaluating/Repairing:* Criteria rewarding the ability to recognize slips of the tongue or audience confusion and actively repairing them in real-time.
    *   *Keywords in Rubrics:* *Menyesuaikan* (adjust), *memperbaiki* (repair/correct), *merespons audiens* (respond to audience), *mengelola kendala* (manage constraints).

### 3.3. Data Analysis and Coding Procedures
To ensure reproducibility (mirroring Mach, 2025):
*   **Conservative Proposition Extraction:** Complex sentences within the UTS/UAS rubrics will be broken down into distinct, single-fact propositions. If a rubric states, *"Mahasiswa dapat menjelaskan materi dengan baik dan menyesuaikan intonasi dengan audiens,"* it will be split into two discrete codes: one for declarative delivery and one for metacognitive regulation.
*   **Quantitative Baseline:** The percentage of declarative criteria vs. regulatory criteria will be calculated per campus to establish a regional baseline for Bandung and Cimahi.
*   **Inter-rater Reliability:** A second researcher will independently code 20% of the gathered rubrics. Discrepancies will be resolved via discussion to establish a strong Cohen's Kappa score.

---

## 4. Addressing Limitations (The Verification Layer)
**Identified Flaw in Mach (2025):** Analyzing rubrics does not guarantee that those documents reflect actual classroom implementation or real-time grading behaviors during the presentation.

**Proposed Mitigation (Within the 4-Month Timeline):**
Given the timeline, a full observational study is not feasible. Instead, a targeted secondary verification step will be implemented:
*   A brief, structured questionnaire will be given to the speaking lecturers (*Dosen Pengampu*) at the 8 target campuses. 
*   The questionnaire will ask them to rank the actual weight they give to "real-time adaptation/regulation" versus "content memorization/theory" when observing live UTS/UAS presentations, allowing the study to contrast *documented rubric policy* with *actual assessment practice*.
